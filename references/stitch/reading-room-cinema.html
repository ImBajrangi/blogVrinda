The Nature of Being · Oct 12

The Silence Between Thoughts

In the quiet spaces that emerge when the mind finally ceases its endless chatter, we find not emptiness, but a profound presence. This presence is the bedrock of our existence, the canvas upon which the painting of our lives is rendered.

Often, we identify so closely with the noise—the anxieties, the plans, the memories—that we believe we are the noise. But consider the sky. Clouds pass through it, sometimes dark and stormy, sometimes light and wispy. Yet, the sky remains untouched, vast, and eternally present behind the weather.

"Do not seek to stop the thoughts. Seek only to recognize the space in which they appear. The space is who you are."

The practice is not one of forceful suppression. It is an act of gentle withdrawal of attention. When a thought arises, instead of following its narrative thread into the labyrinth of past or future, simply observe it as an event occurring in consciousness. It has a beginning, a middle, and an end.

And when it ends, before the next thought begins, there is a gap. A momentary cessation of mental movement. It is in this gap that the truth of our nature is revealed. It is a silence that speaks louder than any words, a stillness that is more dynamic than any action.

As we cultivate the ability to rest in this silence, we begin to realize that peace is not something we must acquire from the outside world. It is our inherent state, temporarily obscured by the busy mind. Returning to this state is not a journey outward, but a relaxing inward.
